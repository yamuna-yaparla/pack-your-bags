Leh-Ladakh – The Land of High Passes

LEH-LADAKH

Begin your journey in Leh, the capital of Ladakh, where you can explore the Leh Palace and Shanti Stupa. The palace offers panoramic views of the town, while the Shanti Stupa is a symbol of peace and tranquility.

Next, head to Pangong Lake, one of the most iconic destinations in Ladakh. The lake's crystal-clear blue waters and surrounding mountains create a surreal landscape. Spend a night by the lake to witness the starry sky and the serene beauty of the place.

Visit Nubra Valley, known for its sand dunes and double-humped camels. The valley is accessible via the Khardung La Pass, one of the highest motorable passes in the world. Enjoy the scenic drive and the unique landscape of the valley.

High Altitude: Ladakh is a high-altitude desert, with much of the region lying above 3,000 meters (9,800 feet). This altitude contributes to its dry, cold climate.

  Mountain Ranges: The region is characterized by rugged mountain ranges, including the Himalayas and the Karakoram.

Climate: Ladakh experiences extreme temperature variations. Summers are short and cool, while winters are long and bitterly cold. The region receives very little rainfall.  

Indus River: The Indus River flows through Ladakh, playing a crucial role in the region's ecosystem.  

Magnetic Hill

Magnetic Hill is a natural wonder where vehicles appear to move uphill on their own. This optical illusion is a must-visit for travelers and offers a unique experience.The surrounding terrain creates an illusion that makes a slight downhill slope appear to be an uphill slope. Therefore, the vehicles are actually rolling downhill, even though it looks like they're moving uphill.

Thiksey Monastery

Thiksey Monastery is one of the most beautiful monasteries in Ladakh, resembling the Potala Palace in Lhasa. The monastery houses a large statue of Maitreya Buddha and offers stunning views of the Indus Valley.Thiksey Monastery (or Thiksey Gompa) is a prominent Tibetan Buddhist monastery of the Gelugpa sect, located in the Ladakh region of northern India. It's situated on a hilltop, offering breathtaking views of the Indus Valley.

Adventure Activities

Ladakh is a paradise for adventure enthusiasts. From trekking in the Markha Valley to river rafting in the Zanskar River, there's something for everyone. The region is also popular for biking and camping.
